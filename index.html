OpenExpense is a privacy-first, open-source financial management tool. Unlike standard platforms that monetize personal data, our offline-first architecture ensures your financial footprint remains entirely under your control.

  • • Data Sovereignty: You own your history. No tracking, no cloud, no profiling.
  • • Full Transparency: Our codebase is open-source and available for audit on GitHub.
  • • Zero-Cost Model: Free, sustainable, and built without subscriptions or data-mining incentives.

Our architecture is built on hostile-environment-ready principles. We remove the network dependency entirely to ensure your financial habits remain private.

  • Zero-Server Architecture: Code runs exclusively on your hardware. There are no remote backends to intercept data.
  • Encrypted at Rest: Your ledger is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it is written to local storage. The key is generated on your device and is non-extractable, so the stored data is unreadable even from the browser's developer tools.
  • Privacy by Design: Without a remote server, your data is physically immune to breaches or corporate harvesting.
  • Total Custody: Your financial life remains strictly between you and your machine.

Data Sovereignty & Privacy

OpenExpense is designed for total data ownership. Because this tool runs entirely in your browser, you are the only person who can access or view your financial records.

How Your Data Is Stored

Your ledger is saved into your browser's IndexedDB and encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it ever touches disk. The encryption key is created on this device, marked non-extractable, and kept inside the database — so even someone inspecting local storage sees only ciphertext.

Autosave writes every change to this encrypted local storage automatically — there are no files to manage and nothing is ever saved as plaintext. It's on by default; the disk button in the header pauses it for an ephemeral session where changes stay in memory only.

Encryption requires a secure context (an https:// address or localhost). On the live site this is always the case. The status chip on the calendar shows whether encryption and autosave are active.

Backups & Moving Between Devices

Click Export to download an encrypted .zip containing two files: ledger.enc.json (your ledger, encrypted with AES-256-GCM) and ledger.key.json (the key that decrypts it). Click Import and select that .zip to restore everything on any device.

You can also load the two files separately — Import the key and the encrypted ledger in either order and OpenExpense joins them automatically. For sensitive backups, keep the key file apart from the encrypted file so neither is useful on its own. Legacy plaintext .json exports still import fine.

Technical Structure

Your data uses a simple, readable dictionary structure. Calendar dates act as unique keys, mapping to your expense objects. This is the shape used in export files and inside the encrypted record.

{ "name": "GBA Expenses", "events": { "2026-06-03": [{ "title": "API Hosting", "price": 49.99, "recurring": true }] } }

Security Notes

  • • Device-bound encryption: The key lives on this device. It protects your stored data from casual inspection and backups, but not from someone with full access to this browser profile.
  • • Backup often: Export your encrypted .zip periodically so a cleared browser or lost device doesn't take your history with it.
  • • Split the set for sensitive data: The key and the encrypted ledger can be stored or sent separately — together they decrypt, apart they don't.
  • • No syncing: Since there is no cloud server, move your export manually when switching computers.
